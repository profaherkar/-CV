      Football

      Football has always been my favourite sport and I have played from a very young age. As of recently, I have been captain of Egerton and Pluckley United for the years 2016-2019 as well as joining my sixth form team 2019-present.
      I have refined the combined skills of leadership and teamwork at different levels as well as a growing confidence within the game.

      Running

      Running has always been a funny sport for me. My family is a running family - parents and sisters - but I never really got into it. However, as a target for my DofE physical section, I decided start running to improve my fitness. This was in 2018. Since then, I have now run the Givaudan 10k in October 2018, the Lydd Half Marathon in March 2019 and three more half marathons and now I am currently training for a 3-hour marathon for April 2020. I like to think this demonstrates by focus and commitment when I start something new and I applied the mixed skills of motivation, resilience and persistence. The rush of nerves before a race is a feeling I'll never get used to but, allows me to easily focus on the task ahead.

      Swimming

      Furthermore, for a four month period I swam swam before school early in the morning to support a friend who was training for multiple swimming cometitions. Although I am no professional swimmer myself, my determination to be there for him, motivated him to give it everything he had. This was one of the first moments I truly understood the concept of time-management - I had to commit to waking up early and getting to the swimming pool, doing a swimming session, and walking to school in time for lessons.

      Cycling

      As a part of my marathon training which I am currently undertaking, on my two off days from running, I am cycling in order to keep my muscles loose due to the consistent tightening after a run. These cycles can range anywhere from 10k to 25k and help get the blood pumping without unnecessary efforts. I also cycle for fun with my sister on the afternoon of a morning run as it it her passion and being a part of what she loves makes us both happy

      Linesman

      My younger sister also plays football, and she has played for Maidstone United for the last couple of years and still going strong! What started as a show of support along the sideline, has now evolved into me being their official linesman for all their matches with the occasional game where I have to step in as referee if their's is unable to make it. I love the fact that it is an all girls team because it shows the universal passion for the sport and being able to support my little sister in such a way helps me as well as her - I need a calm, confident head, a weekly commitment and a good fitness level - another motivation to keep up the running. It also means I get to be a part of her successes and brings us closer as a family.
